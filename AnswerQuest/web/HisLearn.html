  • World War I was a global war that began in 1914 and lasted until 1918. It was one of the deadliest conflicts in history, paving the way for major political changes, including revolutions in many of the nations involved.

  • World War II was a global war that lasted from 1939 to 1945. It involved the vast majority of the world's nations—including all of the great powers—eventually forming two opposing military alliances: the Allies and the Axis.

  • Titanic was a liner that sank in the Atlantic Ocean on 15 Apr 1912 after colliding with an iceberg during her maiden voyage from Southampton to NYC. This resulted in the loss of more than 1,500 passengers and crew.

  • Joseph Stalin (18 Dec 1878 – 5 Mar 1953) was the leader of the Soviet Union from the mid-1920s until his death in 1953.

  • The President of the United States of America is the head of state and head of government of the United States.

  • Adolf Hitler (20 Apr 1889 – 30 Apr 1945) was an Austrian-born German politician who was the leader of the Nazi Party.

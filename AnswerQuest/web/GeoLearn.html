Rivers

Volcanoes

Earthquakes

  • Rivers form part of the water cycle. Water falls from the atmosphere as some form of precipitation. They are responsible for developing their own immediate scenery.

  • For thousands of years volcanoes have created and taken life. They have been studied, worshipped, feared and respected. Even with current technology we are unable to compete with the power of a volcanic eruption.

  • An earthquake is the result of a sudden release of energy in the Earth's crust that creates seismic waves. Earthquakes are one of the most destructive natural hazards on earth. They are difficult to predict and plan for.

  • Glaciation is the effects on land of ice sheets or glaciers that erode rocks and deposit the rock debris.

  • A coastline is the area where land meets the sea or ocean. The term "coastal zone" is a region where interaction of the sea and land processes occurs.

  • A population is a summation of all the organisms of the same group or species, which live in a particular geographical area, and have the capability of interbreeding.
